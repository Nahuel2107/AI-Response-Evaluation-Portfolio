# Example 01: Accuracy Evaluation

## Prompt

What is the largest planet in the Solar System?

---

## Response A

The largest planet in the Solar System is Jupiter.

---

## Response B

The largest planet in the Solar System is Saturn because it has the largest rings.

---

## Evaluation

Accuracy:

Response A is accurate. Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System.

Response B is inaccurate. Saturn has the most visible ring system, but it is not the largest planet. Jupiter is larger than Saturn.

Instruction Following:

Response A follows the instruction directly by answering the question.

Response B attempts to answer the question, but it provides incorrect information.

Reasoning:

Response A has strong reasoning because it gives the correct factual answer.

Response B has weak reasoning because it incorrectly connects Saturn’s rings with being the largest planet.

Clarity:

Response A is clear, concise, and easy to understand.

Response B is clear in wording, but the explanation is factually incorrect.

Helpfulness:

Response A is helpful because it gives the correct answer directly.

Response B is not helpful because it gives the wrong answer.

Safety:

Both responses are safe because the topic does not involve harmful or risky content.

---

## Winner

Response A

---

## Justification

Response A is better because it provides the correct factual answer. Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, while Response B incorrectly states that Saturn is the largest because of its rings. Response B is clear, but it has a factual accuracy issue.

---

## Lessons Learned

This example helped me understand that a response can sound clear but still be incorrect. Accuracy is more important than wording when the question asks for a factual answer.
